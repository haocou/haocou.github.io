Rigid Body Motion

Rigid body motion means that: given any two points ppp and qqq, which must satisfy the following formula

∥p(t)−q(t)∥=∥p(0)−q(0)∥=const\|p(t)-q(t)\|=\|p(0)-q(0)\|=const ∥p(t)−q(t)∥=∥p(0)−q(0)∥=const

Besides, rigid body transformation has the following properties

  • Preserved length. Given mapping g:R3→R3g:\mathbb{R}^3\to \mathbb{R}^3g:R3→R3, ∥g(p)−g(q)∥=∥p−q∥\|g(p)-g(q)\|=\|p-q\|∥g(p)−g(q)∥=∥p−q∥ holds.
  • Preserved cross product. Given mapping g:R3→R3g:\mathbb{R}^3\to \mathbb{R}^3g:R3→R3, g∗(v×ω)=g∗(v)×g∗(ω)g_*(v\times \omega)=g_*(v)\times g_*(\omega)g∗​(v×ω)=g∗​(v)×g∗​(ω)

  • The columns of R∈R3×3R\in\mathbb{R}^{3\times3}R∈R3×3, which is r⃗1,r⃗2,r⃗3∈R3\vec{r}_1,\vec{r}_2,\vec{r}_3 \in \mathbb{R}^3r1​,r2​,r3​∈R3, are mutually orthonormal. It can be written as
